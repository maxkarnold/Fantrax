“All In” on Villa pays off for North London Troopz


By Max Arnold


The premier league didn’t disappoint again this week with goals galore. High scoring games and the controversial handball rule resulted in a difficult week for defenders. The three teams who were top of the league last week lost their games. With no title favorite appearing as of yet, the top should be much closer than last year.

Scary FC vs North London Troopz

42 - 100.5

The reigning champion, North London Troopz, won against Scary FC by a very large margin. An intentional effort to play as many Aston Villa players as possible paid off. His Villa players combined for 60.5 points. Troopz very much maximized his points with his only other double digit scorer being Mahrez (MCI) with 19.5 points. 6 of his starters scored less than 5 points and he’ll have to find another way to win as Villa are playing Liverpool next week. Scary FC will need to make some big moves in order to improve his team which had no standout performers with none scoring in the double digits. With many of his players either borderline starters or low scoring midfield players he should be looking to trade one of his goalkeepers in a package deal for a star player.

Mourinho Out vs 30 Yard Screamers

82.25 - 54.25

Mourinho Out won by 28 points against 30 Yard Screamers with 50 points sitting on his bench. Mourinho Out didn’t have any stand out performers, but had decent performances from Tielemans (LEI), Townsend (CRY), and Kane (TOT). Another lackluster Manchester United performance hurt 30 Yard Screamers with only 6.25 points combined from Martial (MUN) and Wan Bissaka (MUN). He also had 2 non-starters and 0 points from Hélder Costa (LEE) who had been a standout performer for him in past weeks.

Singapore Slingers vs 2 Goals 1 Cup

57.5 - 60

2 Goals 1 Cup got the win against Singapore Slingers in the closest match of the week. Slingers can count himself unlucky as Coady (WOL) had -8 points thanks to West Ham’s thrashing of Wolves. Slingers could have started Lamptey (BHA) who was on his bench with 8.25 points, which would have given him 16.25 more points. Slingers will hope to get more of his players playing as he had 6 players who didn’t play this week. 2 Goals 1 Cup got his first win of the season with an all around balanced performance from his team. The standout performer for him was Lucas (TOT) with 17 points which might be thanks to the looming competition from new Tottenham signing Gareth Bale. He’ll be happy about this win, with five players who didn’t play, including potential game changers in Pulisic (CHE), van Aanholt (CRY), and Redmond (SOU).

PuroAme vs Wan Young McTominaytor

29.5 - 67.5

A very poor week from PuroAme saw him lose to Wan Young Mctominaytor by over 30 points. He had 7 players who didn’t play this week for injuries or other reasons. The players who did start for him were disappointing in general, with -3 points from his star man Raul Jimenez (WOL) and -5.25 points from his goalkeeper Ederson (MCI). On the bright side, he’ll hope that Abraham (CHE) can play more regularly thanks to his goal and a team shake-up from Frank Lampard. Wan Young McTominaytor did well this week with a balanced performance from his team with no real standout performers. Praet (LEI) has been excellent so far this season, but might be getting a real set back thanks to an injury and the reintroduction of Maddison (LEI).

Red Dragons vs AC Aläcrân

96.75 - 74.25

Red Dragons continued a good run of form to beat AC Aläcrân this week in large part due to 42.5 points from star striker Jamie Vardy (LEI). Dragons has some real big star performers on his team with other players Mane (LIV) and Harrison (LEE) scoring 40.5 points combined. Dragons has some large holes in his team with little depth on his bench and -11.5 points from his goalkeeper and two center backs. AC Aläcrân who was previously undefeated can count himself unlucky to face Red Dragons this week. He had the most points of any losing team this week and would have beaten two teams that won this week. A lack of defensive options really cost him this week with only one defender, Alexander-Arnold (LIV), scoring positive points this week. McGinn (AVL) had 21.5 points on his bench and would have made the matchup a lot closer had he started.

So Long GAY BOiii FC vs Maximum Arteta

61.5 - 86

Maximum Arteta took victory against So Long GAY BOiii FC in a close game until the final day. Great performances from both Grealish (AVL) and Zambo Anguissa (FUL) helped take the lead, while poor performances from Willian (ARS), Mitrovic (FUL), and Areola (FUL) sunk S.L.G.B. ‘s hopes of staying top of the league. Maximum Arteta had a lot of decent performances, but had -8.5 points from Mendy (MCI). His top performer was Bruno Fernandes (MUN) who scored a penalty in the last second of the game against Brighton. S.L.G.B. had a lot of good performers on his bench, notably March (BHA) with 21.5 points. S.L.G.B. started Rudiger (CHE) who didn’t play in his game. With so many good players on his team, I expect S.L.G.B. to be one of the main title challengers this season.

SAMURAI BOMBER vs #TindallBall

101 - 26.25

In the most lopsided matchup this week – top scoring team – SAMURAI BOMBER cruised to victory against the lowest scoring team, #TindallBall. #TindallBall started 3 players who didn’t play and had disappointing performances from his star players. The only player on his team to score more than 4 points was Ings (SOU) with 12.5 points. Sterling was especially disappointing playing in his makeshift role up front with 0.5 points. SAMURAI BOMBER was able to get his first win of the season with highest points in the entire league. It was a great overall performance from his team with his one standout performer being Mason Mount (CHE) with 23 points. He’ll hope that Mount keeps up his good form so that this wasn’t a fluke performance. Three injuries to SAMURAI BOMBER’s starting lineup this week will be very disappointing. He should look to pick up the potential backups for these players.
