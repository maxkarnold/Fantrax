Watkins steals the show for Wan Young McTominaytor


By Max Arnold


It was quite an exciting weekend of football as there were two penalty kicks saved and one missed. Aston Villa thrashed Arsenal 3-0 and their great performances have translated into fantasy. Grealish (AVL) and Watkins (AVL) are both in the top ten players with the most points so far. Mourinho Out remained top of the table this week and there were no crazy upsets. Perhaps the best game was a close one between Wan Young McTominaytor and AC Aläcrân.

Wan Young McTominaytor vs AC AC Aläcrân

109.5 - 95

Wan Young McTominaytor can thank a great performance from Ollie Watkins (AVL) in order to stretch his winning streak to four games after defeating AC Aläcrân. Watkins scored 27 points and is now 8th in points among all players with a game in hand. Dan Burn (BHA) had quite the exceptional game at left wing back, scoring 20 points in a makeshift role. Bale had his first start of the season for Tottenham and scored 7 points, it looks like Bale will be starting from now on and should be a strong fantasy asset from here on out. I don't think anyone thought Jota (LIV) would be starting for Liverpool, except for Wan Young McTominaytor. Picking him up was a real steal. You could say McTominaytor managed his matchup perfectly this week with -10.5 points combined on his bench. On AC Aläcrân's bench was Alexander-Arnold, who you'd have to say has been very disappointing so far. Alexander-Arnold was drafted 6th overall in the draft and his performances have been poor. AC Aläcrân actually outscored McTominaytor in the last match with McGinn (AVL) and Barkley (AVL) combining for 30.5 points. One of his best performers was Coufal (WHU) with 17.75 points. Coufal has been fairly consistent so far in having a bad performance, followed by an excellent performance. Benrahma (WHU) was a great play for AC Aläcrân, as he scored 7 points off the bench for West Ham. Unfortunately for him he didn't score enough to beat Wan Young McTominaytor with his other defenders combining for 0.5 points.

Singapore Slingers vs Maximum Arteta

87.75 - 128

Maximum Arteta scored the most points in the league this week to hand Singapore Slingers his third straight loss. Slingers is now 10th in the league, which is astounding since he has scored the fourth most points in the league so far. It's clear that Slingers' luck hasn't been good so far as all the games he's lost were close. Slingers have actually only faced the 7th most points in the league, so his opponents just seem to score more than him recently. Eberechi Eze (CRY) was the highest scoring player for Slingers with 27 points. Eze has been in-and-out of the Crystal Palace starting lineup and a good performance like this might help him secure the spot from his main competiton, Schlupp (CRY). Slingers had Armstrong (SOU) on his bench this week with 25 points, while he had two players with 0 points starting. Perez (LEI) didn't play and Silva (MCI) didn't do much against Liverpool after coming on as a substitute. Son (TOT) hasn't exactly reached the heights of fantasy production he was hoping for after he traded for him. Since the trade, he has not won a game. Slingers boldly played 5 defenders this week, with 3 of the 5 recording clean sheets. Slingers had a decent week, but Maximum Arteta had the best week in the league. A solid backline performance from his keeper and defenders combined for 52.5 points. He also got 30 points from Fernandes (MUN), who is now 5th in fantasy scoring with a game in hand despite Manchester United's struggles this season. News of Pereira (WBA) getting COVID-19 came out this morning before the early game against Tottenham and didn't give Max Arteta much of a chance to switch up the lineup.

SAMURAI BOMBER vs Mourinho Out

72.25 - 87.75

A strong start from SAMURAI BOMBER wasn't enough to defeat league leader, Mourinho Out. In the final game of the weekend, Mourinho Out only needed 0.5 points in order to win. He had a choice between 3 defenders and did excellent to choose the right one. Had he chosen Walker (MCI), who scored -4.5 points thanks to a penalty given up and a yellow card, SAMURAI BOMBER would have won this game. However, that's not what happenedand Mourinho Out was able to win a close one to stay top with another great performance from top scorer Kane (TOT). Kane has 41 points more than the second highest scorer this season (although both Grealish and Fernandes are in the top 5 with a game in hand). Kane scored 23 points this week in a somewhat average performance from Mourniho Out's team. Gross was a good pickup from Mourinho Out, as he has scored in double digits for two straight games. Mourinho Out had Jesus (MCI) coming back from injury, which may have given him selection headaches as he could only play three forwards. Mourinho Out has a lot of talent in that position, perhaps it would be smart to trade one of his forwards for a different position. SAMURAI BOMBER finally got a decent performance from Rashford (MUN), who was one of his 3 players scoring double digits. Manchester United has really struggled this season, after finishing last season so strong. SAMURAI BOMBER's team is really lacking star power right now and Rashford is going to need to put up excellent games for him to win more. SAMURAI BOMBER looks like he's made an excellent pickup in Welbeck (BHA), the forward scored 9.5 points in his first start for Brighton despite no goal involvements.

PuroAme vs #TindallBall

101.25 - 17.75

A red hot PuroAme slaughtered #TindallBall with a fantastic defensive performance. #TindallBall scored the least points in a game this season with 17.75. PuroAme has had some excellent additions to his team recently, with players coming back from injury and gelling into their new teams. PuroAme only started 3 players that he also started in the first week this season (Abraham (CHE), Jimenez (WOL), and Keane (EVE)). Chilwell (LEI) and Reguilon (TOT) have been excellent since their integration into their respective teams. Ziyech has been a superstar so far with two straight 20+ point games. On the other side, #TindallBall played 4 players that didn't start and had subpar performances from the rest of his team. His highest scoring player was McTominay (MUN) with 6 points. #TindallBall actually had 30.75 points on his bench, which outscored his starting team.

So Long GAY BOiii FC vs 30 Yard Screamers

107.25 - 28.75

So Long GAY BOiii FC defeated 30 Yard Screamers with a balanced performance from his entire team. SLGB's best performer was Souček (WHU) with 19.5 points. James Rodriguez scored his lowest amount in a game this week with 2.5, but other players picked up the slack. All his defenders and goalkeeper scored 10+ points as well as two of his midfielders. For 30 Yard Screamers, he had a number of disappointing performances this week. Martial (MUN) came back for his first game from his suspension and was awful. Martial has probably been one of the worst players in the league so far with -3.5 points, while also missing 3 games thanks to a red card suspension. 30 Yard Screamers' best performer was Shaw (MUN) with 11 points. Shaw also picked up a little injury, but this is worrying as Shaw might lose his place in the starting eleven if Telles (MUN) comes in and performs. 30 Yard Screamers now has four injured players and will hope that they can all use the international break to recover.

Red Dragons vs North London Troopz

35 - 53.5

North London Troopz defeated Red Dragons in the lowest scoring game of the week. North London Troopz was fairly lucky to play Red Dragons this week with the fifth worst points in the league. He can thank Fabiánski (WHU) for his penalty save in the 98th minute against Lookman (FUL) for the victory. Fabiánski's 22 points was the most from his team and had he not saved the penalty the game would have been very different. Fabiánski would have had 4 points instead, which would have still seen him won but by only a half point. His other standout performer was Romeu (SOU) with an excellent 13.5 points from midfield. The rest of his team was very poor and one his key players, Mahrez (MCI) was out of the matchday squad for no apparent reason. Zouma (CHE) had a surprisingly poor game this week, as I've mentioned in weeks prior, he has been North London Troopz best performer, but a 3 point performance was definitely a let down. For Red Dragons, it was an equally if not worse performance from him and his team. Red Dragons was missing Aguero and Trossard thanks to injury, but played both of them despite having two other players on his bench he could have played. Had Red Dragons played McNeil (BUR) and Silva (CHE), he would have won the game comfortably. Red Dragons top scorer was Zaha (CRY) with just 10 points followed closely by Mane (LIV) with 9.5 points. Red Dragons may have made a smart decision by not playing Digne (EVE), who scored -2 points this week. That's two losses in a row for Red Dragons, scoring less than 40 points both weeks following his trading away of Vardy (LEI) for Digne (EVE). Improvement is needed, if his team is going to get better this season.
