Mourinho Out secures close victory after trading Son


By Max Arnold


The Premier League has definitely settled down with less goals and better defense as of late. However, this didn't mean there was anything short of drama as many matches were very close going into the last game of the week. Three matchups were still in the balance going into the last game of Tottenham vs Burnley. Two of those games kept Mourinho Out and Maximum Arteta top of the table, both with a record of 5-1.

Singapore Slingers vs Mourinho Out

64.5 - 70

Mourinho Out defeated Singapore Slingers in a matchup that was close until the final game. Before the matchup, Mourinho Out and Singapore Slingers made a big trade. Slingers received Son (TOT) and Egan (SHU), while Mourinho Out received Calvert-Lewin (EVE) and Cash (AVL). Both Cash and Calvert-Lewin played poorly for their standards with a combined 1.5 points between the two of them. Mourinho Out's best performer was Mendy (CHE), Chelsea's new goalkeeper. Mendy has gotten two clean sheets in a row and is looking like an excellent pickup as well as Semedo (WOL) who has scored 8+ points in four consecutive games. For Singapore Slingers, his best performance came out of Batshuayi (CRY) who scored 14 points in his second start of the season. Going into the last game of the week, both teams were neck and neck. In the end it was Tottenham's clean sheet that won the game for Mourinho Out, as Dier (TOT) scored 13.75 points. Had Tottenham conceded, Mourinho Out would have lost the matchup by 1.5 points. Looking forward it looks like Dubravka (NEW) will be out until December, which means Slingers can keep rolling with Darlow (NEW) as his goalkeeper for a while longer.

PuroAme vs Scary FC

67.5 - 68.5

Scary FC defeated PuroAme in the closest matchup of the week. Scary FC has really solidified his team as a defensive powerhouse as his top 3 peformers were his goalkeeper and 2 defenders. However, Digne (EVE), one of his top defenders, received a red card in Everton's calamitous loss to Southampton. Digne, who is usually one of the best defenders in the league fantasy-wise scored -9.5 points. Scary FC won by only one point and can thank Chris Wood's (BUR) performance for the win, scoring 6.5 points. 4.5 of those points came from 9 aerials from the Burnley forward. It was the thinnest of margins and PuroAme didn't help himself out. He had four players who scored 0 points with only one of them starting. He even had a backup to Reguilon (TOT) with Davies (TOT) who he could have played in the last game. For whatever reason, PuroAme didn't start Davies and lost the matchup. However, for PuroAme, he has some great potential in his team. Lookman (FUL) and Fofana (LEI) have both been good since he picked them up, even Fofana is not likely to start once Söyüncü (LEI) comes back. Also, he still has Ndidi (LEI) and Pereira (LEI) to come back from injury and Ziyech (CHE) looked good in Chelsea's 4-0 victory in the Champions League against Krasnodar.

Maximum Arteta vs #TindallBall

66.75 - 58.5

Maximum Arteta managed a narrow victory against #Tindallball with performances from Bruno (MUN) and Adams (SOU). Going into the last game of the week #TindallBall was leading by 4 points with one player left to go. Maximum Arteta had Brownhill (BUR) and Pope (BUR) going into the last game. Pope had an ok game, but it was Brownhill's 10 points that was really key to the victory. An early yellow card for Brownhill didn't stop him as he continued his good form. Brownhill has been one of Maximum Arteta's best pickups since the beginning of the season. One odd part of the matchup was actually Saiss (WOL) who scored a clean sheet worth 6 points, even though his team conceded a goal. Saiss played 68 minutes for Wolves and was subbed off before Newcastle scored the equalizer. Quite a weird situation, that Fantrax still counts as a clean sheet may have been a real difference maker. Had Saiss been given a GAD and no clean sheet, Maximum Arteta would have still won, but only by 0.25 points. On the other end, Maguire (MUN) had another good game, getting his first clean sheet of the season. #TindallBall's best performer was Ings (SOU) with 20.5 points including 2 assists. Ings had a breakout season last year, scoring 22 goals and is currently averaging 13.1 fantasy points per game. Four players for #Tindallball didn't play/start for their respective teams. It would be wise for #TindallBall to make use of his injured reserve next week and pickup a couple more players to ensure his team has more options to come off the bench.

Wan Young McTominaytor vs 30 Yard Screamers

71 - 51.5

Wan Young McTominaytor got another impressive victory without star player De Bruyne (MCI), who actually still scored 4 points without starting for Manchester City. McTominaytor's best peformer this week was actually his goalkeeper Meslier (LEE). The young french goalkeeper was not predicted to start the season for Leeds, but has proven to be a decent goalkeeper. His two other top performers are also young up-and-comers, Saka (ARS) and Jota (LIV). Saka was played in a front three for Arsenal this week and managed to be credited 6 points for a clean sheet, since he was subbed off before Arsenal conceded. Jota got a start for Liverpool this week probably thanks to the numerous injuries in their squad. He was able to score a goal in their win and it should be interesting to see if he can continue to find gametime for Liverpool. 30 Yard Screamers didn't have many good performers this week. Costa (LEE) was his top scorer with 13.5 points and Henderson (LIV) put up an impressive 8.5 points. He'll mostly be disappointed in playing Brady, who didn't play for Burnley, while leaving Baldock (SHU), Antonio (WHU), and Reed (FUL), all of them scored 7+ points. Had 30 Yard screamers played all these players, would he have won his matchup? Well, if he had played it perfectly yes. He would have won by 3 points.

Red Dragons vs SAMURAI BOMBER

101.75 - 52

Red Dragons won a comfortable victory over SAMURAI BOMBER with a big contribution from Zaha (CRY). Red Dragons is proving to not just be lucky this year, but that his team has actual depth. With both Aguero injured this week, scoring 0 points, and Vardy injured before this week and scoring 12 points coming off the bench. Red Dragons has had some bad luck with forwards, but has still managed to achieve more points than anyone this week. His top scorer was Zaha (CRY) with 29.5 points and he had 4 other players in double digits. James (CHE) hasn't managed to start lately, but was able to get in this week when Chelsea played a back 3 with James at wing back. Harrison (LEE) and Koch (LEE) both had exceptional games for Leeds United in their 3-0 victory against Aston Villa. Mata (MUN) started his second league game in a row and time will tell if he continues to start for Manchester United. SAMURAI BOMBER had some good performers this week, but was once again hurt by his team's lack of depth. He played two forwards, Cavani (MUN) and Vinicius (TOT), who didn't start for their respective teams and both gave him 0 points. The van Dijk (LIV) has really hurt SAMURAI BOMBER, along with some other players on his team that seem to be there for sentimental value. SAMURAI BOMBER is going to need to do some waiver wire magic in order to turn his season around.

2 Goals 1 Cup vs So Long GAY BOiii FC

40 - 91.5

So Long GAY BOiii FC was able to get back to winning ways with a victory over 2 Goals 1 Cup. SLGB didn't have any real standout performers this week, but a combined team effort helped him towards a nice point total of 91.5 (third best in the league). He got double digit points from Klich (LEE), Saint-Maximin (NEW), Firmino (LIV), Castagne (LEI), and Lloris (TOT). 3 games in a row, Traore hasn't started for Wolves as he looks to be an impact sub for Nuno Espírito Santo. So far, in fantasy terms, Traore (WOL) has not been good and luckily SLGB has the squad depth to cover. 2 Goals 1 Cup remains bottom of the league table after this defeat. He now has Pulisic (CHE) and Redmond (SOU) coming back from injury. While Ndombele (TOT) continues to start and was 2 Goals 1 Cup's top performer this week.

AC Aläcrân vs North London Troopz

97.75 - 51.25

An amazing hattrick from Bamford (LEE) carried AC Aläcrân to a comfortable victory over North London Troopz. Bamford scored a season high 38.5 points thanks to a hattrick where all the goals came in the second half. Karlan Grant (WBA) a new pickup for AC Aläcrân scored 12.5 points. North London Troopz' best performer was Zouma (CHE), which has been fairly common this season. His top 3 draft picks have been very disappointing this year and combined for a total of 12 points this week.
