Mourinho Out! explodes for almost 150 points thanks to Son and Kane.


By Max Arnold


A very high scoring week in the Premier League saw a large variation in scores throughout the FPL. Highlighted by another great week from So Long Gay Boiii which put him in first place and a point explosion for Mourinho Out!, thanks to 40+ point performances from Son (TOT) and Kane (TOT).

Mourinho Out! vs Scary FC

143.75 - 72

In a massive 71 point victory, “Mourinho Out!” was probably saying “Mourinho In!” as Son (TOT) and Kane (TOT) combined for a whopping 92 points! They were involved in all 5 of Tottenham’s goals. Although he had a great week with other great performances, his defense and goalkeeper combined for only 4.75 points and could be an area to be looked at in the future. Scary FC didn’t do horrible with 72 points with good performances from Ceballos (ARS) and Digne (EVE). He’ll be hopeful Donny van de Beek can be a regular starter going forward for Manchester United.

North London Troopz vs So Long GAY BOiii FC

53.5 - 106

So Long GAY BOiii FC will be thinking “this is the year” after his 50+ point victory over last year’s regular season champion North London Troopz. He had several draft picks that have turned out well for him including Willian (ARS), Klich (LEE), Castagne (LEI), and Mitrovic (FUL). Spending all $100 of his waiver budget on James Rodriguez (EVE) may have been seen as a gamble at the time but it paid off as James scored 15+ points for the second straight week and it took the Colombian international no time to adjust to the premier league and Everton. On the other side, North London Troopz will be disappointed as several players performed below par. Mahrez (MCI), Doucoure (EVE), and Zouma (CHE) combined for 0.5 points. However, his last round draft pick Martinez (AVL) seems to have worked out as the goalkeeper scored 19 points on his debut for his new team. North London Troopz can count himself unlucky for a lot of reasons, but seems unlikely to repeat last year’s dominance.

Maximum Arteta vs PuroAme

42.5 - 55.75

A red card to both Bissouma (BHA) and a -5.25 performance from Lewis (NEW) saw Maximum Arteta fall short in a low scoring affair against PuroAme. Having Diangana (WBA) score 17 points on his bench will have him frustrated he didn’t play him. PuroAme best performer was Chris Wood (BUR) with 15 points thanks to a goal, two shots on target, and four aerials. Ultimately a goal from Raúl Jiménez sealed the win.

#TindallBall vs Red Dragons

81.75 - 85

In the closest game of the week, Red Dragons was led by Zaha (CRY) and Mané, scoring a combined 54 points with 2 goals each. #TindallBall had 3 players over 20 points - Alisson (LIV), Ings (SOU), and Barnes (LEI) - but a red card from Sheffield United defender John Egan and no positive points from any of his other defenders saw him fall short by 3.25 points to Red Dragons.

2 Goals 1 Cup vs Wan Young McTominaytor

29.25 - 94.75

A one sided matchup was exacerbated when De Bruyne (MCI) scored 38 points for Wan Young McTominaytor in the last game of the week. 2 Goals 1 Cup had a very poor week with only Werner (CHE) and Firmino (LIV) scoring over 10 points. Mctominaytor will be pleased with the pickup of Dennis Praet (LEI) who has scored over 10 points in two straight weeks.

30 Yard Screamers vs SAMURAI BOMBER

74.75 - 59

30 Yard Screamers got his second win of the year after getting a fairly close 74.75 - 59 victory. He’ll be happy with a 23 point performance from Hélder Costa (LEE) and a solid 8.5 points from Reed (FUL). Although Martial (MUN) only scored -1.5 points for him, his victory was helped by a Manchester United collapse to Crystal Palace. His opponent SAMURAI BOMBER had Rashford, Fosu-Mensah, and De Gea (all from Manchester United) scoring a combined 7 points. BOMBER’s highest scoring player was Trézéguet (AVL) with only 13 points. He’ll hope for better luck next week as he searches for his first win of the season.

AC Aläcrân vs Singapore Slingers

110 - 74.5

A substantial victory from AC Aläcrân saw him move into second place and in the process sending down the former leader in the standings to fifth. Solid games from all his players saw him scoring 110 points. His lowest scoring player was Sander Berge (SHU) with 4.5. Singapore Slingers had one standout player in Calvert-Lewin (EVE) score 34 points, but didn’t get much from his other players including Salah (LIV) who didn’t manage to get a goal or assist after his hat trick last week.
